## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Azakaw is a modular compliance platform that bundles eKYC within a broader regulatory stack covering AML transaction surveillance, FATCA/CRS reporting, and complaints management. This positions it differently from pure-play identity verification vendors: the value proposition is compliance workflow consolidation rather than best-in-class biometric accuracy. For financial institutions managing multiple regulatory obligations across a fragmented vendor landscape, azakaw's consolidation play is genuinely appealing. Early-stage vendor with no published reviews; buyers should request compliance references in their specific regulatory jurisdiction.

### Best For

Banks, credit unions, wealth managers, and regulated financial platforms in markets where AML, eKYC, and FATCA/CRS obligations overlap. Organizations that currently manage these compliance functions across multiple point solutions will find the most value in azakaw's unified platform approach.

### Avoid If

Companies whose primary requirement is high-accuracy biometric identity verification for fraud prevention or access control. Azakaw's biometric component serves compliance workflow purposes; buyers needing sub-second face matching with deepfake detection should evaluate specialized biometric vendors instead.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — Subscription SaaS model with a documented demo request flow suggests a standard API/webhook integration path. Custom integrations are available at the Enterprise tier, indicating pre-built connectors for common banking core systems may exist.

### Pricing Analysis

Azakaw's published pricing ($49/month Basic, $199/month Pro) is atypically low for enterprise compliance software, suggesting these tiers target small financial firms or RegTech pilots. The Enterprise tier at contact pricing is the realistic purchase vehicle for any regulated institution with compliance obligations at scale.

The modular product structure (eKYC, AML, FATCA/CRS, transaction surveillance sold separately or together) means buyers should map their specific compliance obligations to modules before requesting a quote. A financial institution needing all modules will pay substantially more than the published tiers suggest. Ask for per-module and bundled pricing in the same quote for comparison.

## FAQ

**Does azakaw include biometric verification, or just document-based KYC?**
Azakaw includes eKYC as part of its compliance stack, which typically encompasses both document verification and biometric checks. Confirm with the vendor which biometric modalities (facial recognition, liveness detection) are included versus available as add-ons.

**What AML capabilities does azakaw provide?**
The platform includes transaction surveillance, AI-driven risk scoring, real-time monitoring and alerts, and custom rules configuration. This covers the core AML transaction monitoring obligations for most regulated financial institutions.

**Does azakaw support FATCA and CRS reporting?**
Yes. FATCA/CRS compliance is listed as a dedicated module, which is uncommon among competitors that focus purely on KYC. Financial institutions with cross-border account holders will find this particularly valuable.

**What jurisdictions does azakaw cover for compliance requirements?**
Azakaw is headquartered in New York but serves clients across multiple jurisdictions. Buyers should confirm which specific regulatory frameworks (FCA, FinCEN, MAS, etc.) the platform's compliance rules are updated for.

**Is azakaw suitable for crypto exchanges under Travel Rule requirements?**
The platform's AML and transaction surveillance modules are relevant for VASP compliance. Confirm with the vendor whether Travel Rule data collection and transmission capabilities are included or require additional configuration.
