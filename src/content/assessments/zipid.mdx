## PrimeBiometry Assessment

ZipID is one of the few identity verification products built explicitly around US I-9 employment eligibility requirements — a regulatory obligation that affects every US employer but is often handled with inadequate identity assurance. The product's founding by a former Homeland Security Director signals both deep regulatory expertise and an institutional understanding of the fraud patterns that I-9 verification is meant to prevent. NIST-validated 1:1 facial recognition with 99.8% claimed accuracy sets a credible technical foundation. As an early-stage vendor (founded 2020) with no published reviews, buyers should request reference customers in comparable hiring contexts before deployment.

### Best For

US-based HR and compliance teams at mid-market and enterprise employers who need to strengthen I-9 verification beyond the standard manual document review. Particularly valuable for remote hiring workflows where in-person document inspection is impractical.

### Avoid If

Non-US organizations or companies needing a general-purpose identity verification platform covering KYC/AML, fraud prevention, or multi-jurisdiction compliance requirements. ZipID's I-9 specialization is a strength for its target use case and a limitation outside it.

### Integration Complexity

**Medium** — The product appears to be a standalone application rather than an API-first platform. Integration with HR systems (ATS, HRIS) likely requires custom work or file-based data exchange. Buyers should confirm API availability and HRIS integration connectors during evaluation.

### Pricing Analysis

ZipID lists a Starter plan at $7.50/month — unusually low for an enterprise identity verification product. This likely reflects either a per-seat model (per HR user, not per verification) or a minimum tier for small employers. At scale, organizations hiring hundreds of employees per month will need to understand per-verification costs or volume licensing terms.

US employers should compare ZipID's total cost against the risk exposure of inadequate I-9 compliance. USCIS fines for I-9 violations range from $281 to $2,789 per paperwork violation as of 2026, making even a modestly priced product cost-effective if it substantially reduces compliance risk.

## FAQ

**What is I-9 verification and why does it require specialized software?**
Form I-9 is the US federal document that verifies employment eligibility. Employers must verify both identity and work authorization documents for every new hire. Traditional I-9 processes rely on manual document inspection, which is prone to fraud — ZipID adds NIST-validated facial biometrics to confirm the person presenting documents matches the identity documents provided.

**Is ZipID approved for E-Verify integration?**
E-Verify is a separate DHS system for confirming employment eligibility. Buyers should confirm whether ZipID's output is compatible with E-Verify submission requirements, as E-Verify mandates specific data formats and timing requirements.

**What NIST standard does ZipID's facial recognition meet?**
ZipID claims NIST-validated 1:1 facial recognition at 99.8% accuracy. Buyers should request the specific NIST FRVT submission reference to verify the benchmark conditions are representative of real-world I-9 use cases.

**Does ZipID work for remote I-9 completion?**
The product is positioned for remote hiring workflows, where traditional in-person I-9 document inspection is impractical. Confirm with the vendor whether the remote completion process satisfies DHS remote I-9 authorization requirements.

**What happens if a verification fails or is inconclusive?**
Buyers should understand the exception workflow: what happens when facial recognition cannot confirm identity? A clear human review escalation path is essential for compliance — rejecting all inconclusive results would create disparate impact risk.
