## PrimeBiometry Assessment

EduReg by Synel is a narrow vertical product, purpose-built for UK educational institutions that must meet Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) and Building Safety Act obligations. The biometric attendance use case — fingerprint and facial recognition for student registration — is operationally distinct from commercial identity verification: the buyers are bursars and IT managers, not security officers, and the primary concern is safeguarding compliance, not fraud prevention. With only 1 published review (rating 2/5), independent validation of the product experience is thin.

### Best For

UK secondary schools, sixth forms, and higher education institutions that need automated biometric attendance with audit trails meeting KCSIE requirements. Particularly suited to schools already using Synel's access control hardware, where EduReg extends an existing vendor relationship.

### Avoid If

Any organization outside the UK education sector. EduReg's compliance certifications and feature design are built around UK-specific educational regulations that have no equivalent in commercial or international markets.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — Web-based solution with no complex SDK integration required. The platform includes visitor management and access control as modular additions, suggesting a unified deployment with Synel's hardware ecosystem. Typical school deployment is managed by Synel's team.

### Pricing Analysis

EduReg publishes tiered pricing at £10/user/month (Basic) and £25/user/month (Pro), with enterprise pricing on application. For a secondary school with 1,000 students, Basic tier would cost £10,000/month — which is atypically high for education software. Buyers should treat the published per-user prices as a starting point and negotiate site-wide licensing based on student population.

The Enterprise tier includes customized solutions and dedicated support, which is the appropriate buying vehicle for most school installations. Contact Synel directly to discuss whether per-student or per-site pricing is available, as the listed per-user model is likely not how the product is sold in practice.

## FAQ

**Is EduReg compliant with UK data protection law regarding children's biometric data?**
EduReg is GDPR compliant and specifically designed for UK education environments. UK schools using biometric systems must obtain parental consent under the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012 — EduReg should support the workflows for this, but buyers should confirm the consent management features before deployment.

**Does EduReg support both fingerprint and facial recognition?**
Yes. EduReg supports both biometric modalities for student attendance registration, allowing institutions to choose the method appropriate for their environment.

**What happens if a student or parent refuses biometric registration?**
UK law requires schools to provide a non-biometric alternative for students whose parents object. EduReg's web-based registration provides a fallback mechanism for manual or card-based attendance.

**Does EduReg integrate with existing school management systems?**
Synel's platform includes integrations for attendance data export, but buyers should confirm compatibility with their specific MIS (Management Information System) such as SIMS, Arbor, or iSAMS during the evaluation process.

**Who provides hardware for the biometric terminals?**
Synel is both a hardware and software vendor. EduReg is designed to work with Synel's biometric reader hardware, which creates both a streamlined single-vendor experience and a degree of lock-in. Factor hardware costs into total cost of ownership.
