## PrimeBiometry Assessment

The acquisition of Onfido by Entrust in 2024 fundamentally changed the product's market positioning. Onfido was a pure-play identity verification startup with developer-friendly pricing and a well-regarded API; Entrust is a 55-year-old enterprise security company with $100M+ in funding and a portfolio spanning PKI infrastructure, card and ID issuance, post-quantum cryptography, and certificate lifecycle management. The combined entity is now the most compelling option for large enterprises and government agencies that need to connect digital identity verification to physical credential issuance (passports, national IDs, employee badges) within a single vendor relationship. With 111 G2 reviews at a 4.4 rating, it carries the strongest review depth in this batch of vendors.

### Best For

Government agencies, large enterprises (1,000+ employees), and regulated industries (healthcare, education, telecommunications) that need identity verification as part of a broader enterprise security architecture — specifically organizations that also require card and ID issuance, certificate management, or post-quantum cryptography readiness within a unified vendor relationship.

### Avoid If

Developers or growth-stage companies who valued Onfido's original self-service model and transparent pricing. Entrust operates entirely on enterprise sales cycles with no published pricing. The platform's breadth is a cost center for buyers who only need IDV — Veriff or Sumsub will deliver faster time-to-production and better unit economics for pure digital onboarding.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — REST API, Mobile SDK (iOS/Android), Web SDK, and cloud solutions are all available. Entrust's enterprise heritage means deep documentation and professional services support for integration. However, the full platform's breadth (PKI, certificate management, card issuance) means implementation scoping is more complex than a standalone IDV product.

### Pricing Analysis

Entrust IDV operates on a fully custom enterprise pricing model with no published rates. All tiers (Basic, Standard, Enterprise) are sales-quoted. This is consistent with Entrust's positioning as a strategic security vendor rather than a transactional API service. Expect minimum contract values in the tens of thousands of dollars annually for any meaningful deployment.

For companies evaluating Entrust IDV purely for identity verification, the honest comparison is against what Onfido used to cost as a standalone product. Entrust's value proposition only justifies its pricing premium when the broader security portfolio creates genuine consolidation savings. If your procurement involves certificate lifecycle management, PKI, or physical ID issuance alongside digital IDV — Entrust's bundle pricing becomes competitive. If you only need digital IDV, evaluate Veriff or Sumsub first and use Entrust's quote as a benchmark for negotiation leverage.

## FAQ

**What happened to Onfido? Is it still available as a product?**
Onfido was acquired by Entrust in 2024 and rebranded as Entrust IDV. The underlying identity verification technology continues, but it is now sold as part of Entrust's broader enterprise security portfolio. Existing Onfido customers have been transitioned to Entrust account management. New customers buy Entrust IDV, not Onfido.

**What is post-quantum cryptography and why does Entrust offer it alongside IDV?**
Post-quantum cryptography refers to encryption algorithms that are resistant to attacks from quantum computers, which are expected to break current RSA and ECC encryption within the next decade. Entrust offers it as part of its PKI portfolio for enterprises preparing their security infrastructure for the quantum era. This is relevant for government and defense customers, not for most commercial IDV buyers.

**Does Entrust IDV support physical card and ID issuance?**
Yes. Card and ID issuance is a core Entrust product covering government-issued IDs, employee badges, and payment cards. This integration between digital identity verification and physical credential production is the primary differentiator versus pure-play IDV vendors.

**How does Entrust IDV's iBeta PAD certification compare to Veriff's?**
Both Entrust IDV and Veriff hold iBeta PAD certification for liveness detection. The underlying Onfido liveness technology was well-regarded before the acquisition, and Entrust has maintained this certification post-integration.

**What industries does Entrust IDV serve beyond fintech?**
Entrust serves government, financial services, healthcare, telecommunications, education, and travel. The government and enterprise segments are where Entrust's differentiation from startup IDV vendors is strongest, given its physical credential issuance, PKI, and long-term enterprise contracts in those sectors.
