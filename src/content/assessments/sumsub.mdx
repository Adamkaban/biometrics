## FAQ

**Does Sumsub support the FATF Travel Rule for crypto exchanges?**
Yes. Travel Rule compliance is available in the Enterprise tier, covering the FATF requirement for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) to share originator and beneficiary information for crypto transactions above threshold amounts. This is a hard regulatory requirement for licensed crypto exchanges in most jurisdictions and a key differentiator for Sumsub in the crypto vertical.

**What is "Reusable KYC" in Sumsub's Basic tier?**
Sumsub ID enables users who have been verified once to reuse their verified identity credential across multiple platforms in the Sumsub network, reducing friction for repeat onboarding. This is similar to AU10TIX's Reusable Digital ID — relevant for ecosystems where users move between multiple Sumsub-powered products.

**How does Sumsub's device intelligence feature work?**
Device Intelligence analyzes device fingerprints, signals, and behavioral patterns to detect fraudulent devices — emulators, rooted phones, and devices previously associated with fraud attempts — before a user even submits identity documents. Combined with email and phone risk assessment, this provides a pre-KYC fraud signal layer that reduces costly document verification attempts on high-risk sessions.

**Does Sumsub support KYB (Know Your Business) for corporate onboarding?**
Yes. Business verification is a core product covering UBO identification, company registry checks across multiple jurisdictions, and ongoing due diligence for legal entities. This is particularly relevant for fintech and crypto platforms onboarding institutional clients alongside retail users.

**How does Sumsub compare to Veriff for overall compliance coverage?**
Veriff has stronger published accuracy metrics (99.6% vs. Sumsub's 99.5%) and broader country coverage (230+ countries with iBeta-certified liveness). Sumsub's advantage is compliance breadth — transaction monitoring, Travel Rule, device intelligence, and email/phone risk are not available from Veriff. For pure IDV accuracy, Veriff leads; for end-to-end compliance automation in a single contract, Sumsub is the stronger choice.
