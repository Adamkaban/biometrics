## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Biometric Security by Smart Eye Technology occupies a distinct niche at the intersection of e-signature and biometric authentication — a combination few vendors address. The product's core value proposition is identity assurance for document signing: instead of email-link signatures that verify nothing, the platform ties a signature event to a biometric verification. This matters for legal admissibility, regulated document workflows, and environments where non-repudiation is a compliance requirement. Founded in 1997, Smart Eye brings operational maturity unusual among biometric vendors, though the single published review (5/5) provides minimal basis for independent validation.

### Best For

Legal firms, financial advisors, insurance companies, and compliance teams that need provably verified document signatures. The screen privacy feature adds value for regulated environments where sensitive documents must be protected from shoulder-surfing or screen capture.

### Avoid If

Teams building high-volume KYC pipelines or customer onboarding flows at scale. Biometric Security is optimized for document-centric workflows, not API-driven identity verification. Organizations needing bulk verification throughput should evaluate purpose-built KYC vendors instead.

### Pricing Analysis

Biometric Security uses flat monthly pricing: $29/month (Basic), $99/month (Pro), and contact-for-enterprise. The Basic tier covers core biometric authentication and secure file sharing — suitable for small teams evaluating the product. The Pro tier adds custom branding and priority support, appropriate for teams deploying to clients.

At $29–$99/month with no per-verification fees, the pricing model is predictable and budget-friendly for small to mid-market legal and financial teams. Enterprise buyers needing SSO, custom integrations, or volume commitments should engage the sales team; the flat tiers are clearly designed for SMB, and enterprise pricing will be negotiated separately.

## FAQ

**How does Biometric Security differ from DocuSign or Adobe Sign?**
Standard e-signature platforms verify signers via email link or knowledge-based authentication, which provides limited identity assurance. Biometric Security adds facial biometric verification at the point of signing, creating a stronger audit trail for legal admissibility and non-repudiation.

**What does "screen privacy" mean in this context?**
Smart Eye Technology's screen privacy feature uses computer vision to detect when unauthorized viewers are looking at the screen and can blur or lock the display. This is relevant for regulated environments where sensitive documents cannot be viewed by third parties.

**Is biometric data stored by Smart Eye Technology?**
The product description emphasizes end-to-end encryption and user privacy controls. Buyers should request the Data Processing Agreement and clarify whether biometric templates are stored, and if so, where and for how long.

**Does Biometric Security integrate with document management systems?**
The Enterprise tier includes custom integrations, suggesting DMS integration is possible but requires a direct conversation with the vendor. Basic and Pro tiers appear to be standalone.

**Is Biometric Security HIPAA compliant?**
No HIPAA certification is listed. Healthcare organizations requiring HIPAA-compliant document workflows should verify with Smart Eye Technology whether the platform can be configured to meet HIPAA requirements before purchase.
