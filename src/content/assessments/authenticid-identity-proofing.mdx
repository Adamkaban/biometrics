## FAQ

**What does "identity intelligence" mean in AuthenticID's context?**
AuthenticID's identity intelligence approach combines automated document forensics (detecting altered or fraudulent identity documents) with biometric verification to create a layered fraud detection capability — the goal is identifying not just whether a document is real, but whether it belongs to the person presenting it.

**Is AuthenticID suitable for high-volume consumer onboarding?**
AuthenticID's platform handles automated document analysis, but without published throughput benchmarks or volume-based pricing tiers, buyers should explicitly validate high-volume performance during a POC rather than assuming parity with purpose-built KYC-as-a-service platforms.

**What types of identity documents does AuthenticID analyze?**
AuthenticID's fraud prevention focus suggests support for a broad document library including passports, national IDs, and driver's licenses. Buyers should request the specific supported document list and accuracy benchmarks by document type during evaluation.

**What is the status of AuthenticID's acquisition and who acquired them?**
AuthenticID is listed as "Acquired" in available data. Buyers should confirm the acquiring entity, the impact on the product roadmap, and whether support and pricing structures have changed as a result of the acquisition.

**How does AuthenticID compare to biometric-first vendors like Veriff or Onfido?**
AuthenticID's 25-year history gives it deep document forensics expertise, which may surpass newer vendors in specific fraud detection scenarios. However, newer vendors typically have more modern developer tooling, transparent pricing, and larger review bases. The evaluation should specifically benchmark document fraud detection accuracy on the document types most relevant to your use case.
