## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Biometric.Vision is a Kazakhstan-based facial recognition and e-KYC platform with GDPR and ISO 27001 certification -- an unusual compliance posture for a vendor headquartered in Central Asia. Its NIST and ISO 30107-3 certifications signal genuine investment in international biometric standards, not just local market compliance theater. The platform emphasizes high customizability across industries, suggesting a services-heavy delivery model rather than pure SaaS. With zero published reviews, buyers are evaluating an unvalidated vendor with solid-on-paper certifications. Early-stage vendor with limited published reviews.

### Best For

Financial institutions, telecoms, or government agencies in CIS, Central Asian, or emerging markets that need a locally-deployable, GDPR-compliant facial recognition and e-KYC solution with deep customization capability and are comfortable with sales-negotiated pricing.

### Avoid If

Organizations that need immediate self-serve onboarding, published pricing transparency, or third-party review evidence before initiating a procurement process. Western fintechs with established vendor ecosystems (Jumio, Onfido, iDenfy) will find better-validated options.

### Pricing Analysis

All pricing is contact-sales only, with two tiers described: Standard Plan and Custom Solutions -- both at negotiated rates. This model is typical for vendors targeting enterprise and government contracts, where deal size justifies custom commercial terms.

For buyers in LATAM, EU, or North American markets, the absence of published pricing creates a higher friction evaluation process. Expect pricing proposals to be volume-based and tied to a multi-year contract. If operating in CIS markets, Biometric.Vision's regional presence may yield competitive terms compared to Western vendors with APAC or global pricing structures. Request a sandbox environment alongside the commercial proposal -- the free trial URL suggests one exists.

## FAQ

**Where is Biometric.Vision headquartered and does this affect data residency?**
Biometric.Vision is headquartered in Kazakhstan. Organizations with EU data subjects or GDPR obligations should explicitly confirm data processing agreements and the location of data storage infrastructure before deployment.

**What biometric standards does Biometric.Vision comply with?**
The platform holds GDPR compliance, ISO 27001 certification, and NIST and ISO 30107-3 (Presentation Attack Detection) standards -- the latter being the key anti-spoofing benchmark for liveness detection.

**Is Biometric.Vision suitable for AML screening in addition to KYC?**
AML compliance is listed as a feature within the Standard Plan, suggesting watchlist screening capability. The depth of AML coverage (PEP lists, sanctions databases, update frequency) should be verified during vendor evaluation.

**Does Biometric.Vision offer a free trial?**
Yes, a self-registration trial portal is available at cabinet.biometric.kz for initial evaluation.

**How customizable is Biometric.Vision compared to competitors?**
Biometric.Vision explicitly positions high customizability as a differentiator, targeting industries with specific UI and workflow requirements. This is more relevant for embedded deployments than for organizations wanting a turnkey product with minimal configuration.
