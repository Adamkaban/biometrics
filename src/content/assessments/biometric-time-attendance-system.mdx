## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Cuckoo Tech's Biometric Time Attendance System occupies a specific operational niche distinct from most vendors in this comparison space: its core use case is workforce management, not customer-facing KYC or identity verification for onboarding. The product integrates biometric hardware (fingerprint readers, facial recognition terminals) with attendance tracking software to eliminate buddy-punching, manual timesheets, and access control gaps. For HR and operations buyers, this is meaningfully different from the developer-API-first tools that dominate the biometric software category. With $10M in Private Equity backing and a founding year of 2008, it has organizational maturity.

### Best For

Mid-size employers across manufacturing, healthcare, and retail sectors that need to replace manual attendance systems with biometric verification, integrating directly with payroll and HR platforms — particularly where physical access control and shift management run alongside each other.

### Avoid If

Compliance and security teams evaluating customer identity verification (KYC), account onboarding, or fraud prevention platforms — this product's identity verification capabilities are secondary to its workforce management core, and it lacks the regulatory depth of purpose-built KYC vendors.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — REST API, Mobile SDK (iOS/Android), Web SDK, Java SDK, and Python SDK are available, providing strong developer options across common backend stacks. Both cloud-based and on-premise deployment options are supported, which is critical for organizations with physical hardware infrastructure requirements.

### Pricing Analysis

Cuckoo Tech publishes starter pricing at $29/month (Basic) and $49/month (Pro), with custom enterprise pricing for larger deployments. For organizations managing 50-200 employees, the published tiers offer predictable monthly costs, but the flat-rate structure may not scale economically for enterprises with hundreds or thousands of tracked workers.

For large deployments — particularly those combining software licensing with biometric hardware procurement — the enterprise custom track is the relevant path. Buyers should negotiate hardware bundle pricing alongside software licensing, as per-unit hardware costs can significantly affect total deployment cost at scale.

## FAQ

**Does the system support facial recognition in addition to fingerprint biometrics?**
Yes. The product supports both facial recognition and fingerprint-based identification for time and attendance, allowing organizations to choose the modality appropriate for their workplace environment (e.g., facial recognition is preferred in environments where gloves are worn).

**Can the attendance data integrate directly with payroll systems?**
Integration with payroll and HR platforms is a core capability. Buyers should confirm which specific payroll systems are natively supported versus which require custom API integration during the evaluation process.

**What happens if biometric hardware goes offline — is there a fallback?**
On-premise deployment options suggest local processing capability, but the specific offline fallback behavior (PIN, card, or supervisor override) should be confirmed with the vendor, as it varies by hardware configuration and affects business continuity planning.

**Is the system suitable for multi-site deployments?**
The enterprise tier supports dedicated account management and custom integrations, which implies multi-site support. Organizations with distributed locations across multiple time zones should verify centralized dashboard capabilities and network requirements for hardware-to-software connectivity.

**How is biometric data stored and who owns it under GDPR?**
As a GDPR-certified vendor, Cuckoo Tech supports employer-as-data-controller models. However, given that employee biometric data is categorized as special category data under GDPR Article 9, buyers in the EU should request specific data processing agreements and confirm local storage or data residency options.
