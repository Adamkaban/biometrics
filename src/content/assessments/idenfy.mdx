## PrimeBiometry Assessment

iDenfy stands out in the crowded KYC market by combining automated document verification with human review fallback, making it one of the few vendors that can handle edge cases without a full engineering escalation. Its transparent per-verification pricing removes the guesswork that plagues enterprise contracts, while the SDK coverage (iOS, Android, Web) means faster integration cycles than most competitors.

### Best For

Fintech startups and neobanks needing fast KYC onboarding with under 500 verifications per month. The free trial with real verifications — not just sandbox data — lets engineering teams validate accuracy before committing budget.

### Avoid If

Enterprise with complex multi-jurisdiction requirements. At high volume, per-verification pricing scales unfavorably compared to flat-rate enterprise alternatives like Jumio or Onfido.

## FAQ

**Does iDenfy support passive liveness detection?**
Yes. iDenfy uses both passive and active liveness checks depending on risk configuration. Passive liveness runs without user prompts; active liveness requires the user to perform a head movement. Both modes are configurable per flow.

**What documents does iDenfy verify?**
iDenfy supports 3,000+ document types across 200+ countries, including passports, national IDs, and driver's licenses. Document authentication includes MRZ reading, NFC chip reading (for supported passports), and forgery detection.

**How accurate is iDenfy's ID verification?**
iDenfy reports 99%+ accuracy on supported documents in controlled conditions. Edge cases (poor lighting, damaged documents) are routed to human review, which typically resolves within 2 minutes during business hours.

**Is there a free trial?**
Yes — iDenfy offers 500 free identity verifications with no credit card required. The trial uses the production system, not a sandbox, so results reflect real-world accuracy.

**Does iDenfy offer on-premise deployment?**
No. iDenfy is cloud-only (hosted in EU data centers). For organizations requiring on-premise processing, consider Aware or BioID instead.
