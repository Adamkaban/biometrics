### Integration Complexity

**Low** — Mity provides tiered plans (Starter/Pro/Enterprise) with a self-serve model starting at $29/month. The product is positioned for quick onboarding, and the pricing structure implies API-based integration without requiring enterprise implementation services.

### Pricing Analysis

Mity's published pricing is competitive at entry level: $29/month (Starter) includes basic identity verification, liveness detection, and address verification. The $99/month Pro tier adds passwordless authentication and reverification -- useful for returning user workflows. Enterprise is contact-based.

Given the extremely small team size (2-10 employees), buyers should evaluate vendor sustainability before building production dependencies on this product. For organizations willing to accept early-adopter risk, the low price point and broad feature set at the Starter tier offers good value for pilot testing. However, negotiate clear data portability provisions and escrow terms into any multi-year Enterprise contract to mitigate continuity risk.

## FAQ

**What does Mity mean by "4-dimensional biometric authentication"?**
The "4-dimensional" framing is not a standard industry term. Based on product context, it likely refers to a combination of facial recognition, liveness detection, behavioral signals, and device intelligence as verification layers. Request a technical architecture briefing to confirm what dimensions are actually measured.

**Does Mity's hardware-independent liveness work on low-end smartphones?**
Hardware-independence is a stated differentiator -- the system claims to adapt to poor lighting and lower camera quality. This should be stress-tested during evaluation with your target device population before production deployment.

**Is Mity the same as Mitek Systems?**
No. The scraped website data references miteksystems.com, which appears to be a data error. Mity is a separate, unrelated product operating at ibera.run. Mitek Systems is a well-established, publicly traded identity verification company.

**What is the difference between Mity's Starter and Pro tiers?**
Starter ($29/month) covers basic liveness and address verification. Pro ($99/month) adds passwordless authentication and reverification, which allows returning users to re-authenticate without a full identity check -- reducing friction in repeat-access flows.

**Given Mity's small team, what are the vendor continuity risks?**
With 2-10 employees, Mity carries higher continuity risk than established vendors. Evaluate whether the product roadmap, support SLA, and uptime guarantees are contractually backed. Request reference customers and confirm production uptime metrics before dependency.
