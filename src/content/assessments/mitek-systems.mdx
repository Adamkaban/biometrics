## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Mitek Systems occupies a unique position in the identity verification market: it is one of the few vendors that combines document-based identity verification with check fraud detection (its MiPass and Mobile Deposit products) under one platform. Founded in 1985 and now with 500-1,000 employees, Mitek has decades of financial services pedigree that pure-play IDV startups cannot match. This history gives it credibility in banking and healthcare RFPs where vendor longevity and institutional relationships matter. However, it operates in the shadow of faster-moving competitors (Veriff, Sumsub, AU10TIX) on product velocity and self-service accessibility.

### Best For

North American banks, credit unions, and financial institutions that need both identity verification and check fraud detection in a single vendor relationship — particularly those already using Mitek's Mobile Deposit product and looking to expand into digital onboarding with familiar account management.

### Avoid If

Companies outside North American banking who need transparent, volume-based pricing for digital onboarding at scale. Mitek's enterprise-only commercial model requires sales negotiation, and its product surface area is narrower than competitors like Sumsub or Veriff for non-banking verticals.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — REST API, Mobile SDK (iOS/Android), and Web SDK are all available. As a company with a 40-year legacy of enterprise software deployments, Mitek's integration documentation is mature and includes dedicated technical support for onboarding.

### Pricing Analysis

Mitek operates on a fully custom enterprise pricing model with no published rates. This is consistent with its positioning as a banking-sector vendor where contract values are large and multiyear commitments are standard. For prospects, this means the sales cycle is longer (expect 4-8 weeks for a formal proposal), but it also means pricing is negotiable based on verification volume, product bundle, and contract length.

For financial institutions already using Mitek Mobile Deposit, the most cost-effective path is to negotiate an expansion to IDV within the existing contract. New customers should benchmark Mitek's per-verification economics against Veriff's published Essential tier ($0.80/verification) to establish a reference point in negotiations. The AI-driven fraud detection claim ("solutions evolve with fraud tactics") is a differentiator worth validating with reference customers before committing.

## FAQ

**What is MiPass and how does it differ from standard identity verification?**
MiPass is Mitek's passwordless authentication product that uses biometrics to re-verify users after initial onboarding, enabling continuous authentication without passwords. It combines liveness detection with device enrollment, making it relevant for banking apps that need ongoing session security beyond the initial KYC check.

**Does Mitek support check fraud detection alongside identity verification?**
Yes. Mobile Deposit is Mitek's established product for detecting check fraud in mobile banking apps. This dual capability — IDV + check fraud — in a single vendor is a key differentiator for US banks seeking to consolidate their fraud stack.

**Is Mitek suitable for healthcare identity verification under HIPAA?**
Mitek targets healthcare as a vertical, but HIPAA compliance is not listed in their published certifications. Organizations with strict HIPAA requirements should confirm current certification status directly with Mitek during the procurement process.

**What is the typical implementation timeline for Mitek's identity verification API?**
Mitek offers REST API, Web SDK, and Mobile SDK integrations. For standard API implementations, a 2-4 week technical integration is typical for teams with existing API experience. Enterprise deployments with custom workflows may require additional time.

**How does Mitek compare to Veriff or Sumsub for high-volume digital onboarding?**
Veriff and Sumsub offer more transparent self-service pricing and broader geographic document coverage. Mitek's advantage is in North American banking-specific features (check fraud, financial services compliance depth) and its established relationships with US financial institutions. For pure-play digital onboarding outside banking, Veriff or Sumsub typically offer faster time-to-production.
