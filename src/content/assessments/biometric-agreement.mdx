## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Biometric Agreement occupies a narrow but defensible niche: biometric-enforced electronic signatures with video recording of the signing moment, designed to produce court-admissible attribution evidence. Operating from Mexico and compliant with NOM 151 (the Mexican electronic records standard) alongside GDPR and UNCITRAL, this product addresses a specific legal evidentiary problem that generic e-signature tools like DocuSign do not solve. With a 3/5 rating from one published review, the data on user satisfaction is minimal, but the compliance pedigree for LATAM-facing deployments is genuine.

### Best For

Legal, financial, and compliance teams in Mexico and LATAM markets that need e-signatures backed by biometric identity attribution -- specifically where NOM 151 and CNBV regulatory requirements apply, such as financial services contracts, loan agreements, or regulated HR onboarding.

### Avoid If

Organizations operating exclusively in US, EU, or APAC jurisdictions where NOM 151 compliance is irrelevant and more widely validated e-signature + identity tools like DocuSign with ID verification add-ons already cover the use case.

### Integration Complexity

**Low** — Biometric Agreement provides REST API access and web-based flows. The pricing page structure and plan tiers suggest a self-serve onboarding model. Integration for standard document signing flows should be achievable within 1-2 weeks for a mid-size dev team.

### Pricing Analysis

Pricing is tiered per-user per-month: Basic at $29/month (1 user, 10 verifications), Pro at $79/month (5 users, 50 verifications), and Enterprise at custom pricing. The per-user model works well for small legal or compliance teams running limited monthly signature volumes.

However, the verification caps at each tier are restrictive. At the Basic plan, 10 verifications/month is suitable only for individual practitioners or proof-of-concept testing. The Pro plan's 50 verifications/month will be outgrown quickly by teams processing even moderate contract volumes. For organizations expecting 200+ signed documents per month, push directly to the Enterprise tier negotiation -- the fixed-tier economics break down at moderate scale.

## FAQ

**What makes Biometric Agreement different from standard e-signature tools?**
Unlike tools that only verify an email address, Biometric Agreement binds the signature to a biometric profile (facial recognition and fingerprint) and records a video of the signing moment. This creates court-admissible evidence linking a specific individual to a specific document at a specific time.

**Is Biometric Agreement compliant with Mexican financial regulations?**
Yes. The product is explicitly compliant with NOM 151, CNBV CUB (banking supervision regulations), and CNSF CUSF (insurance supervision standards), making it suitable for regulated financial product agreements in Mexico.

**Can Biometric Agreement be used for GDPR-regulated signatories in the EU?**
GDPR compliance is listed as a certification. However, organizations with EU data subjects should conduct formal review of data residency practices, since the company is Mexico-based and the primary compliance framework is Mexican law.

**What happens to the signed package after completion?**
Each signed agreement is distributed to each signatory as a unique digital file. The video recording and audit trail are stored as part of the tamper-evident package.

**Is there a minimum contract or minimum monthly spend?**
The Basic plan at $29/month has no minimum commitment implied in published pricing. However, Enterprise contracts will have custom minimums. Clarify minimum term length and overage rates before signing any Enterprise agreement.
