## FAQ

**What is a Qualified Trust Service Provider (QTSP) and why does it matter?**
A QTSP is a trust service provider formally accredited by an EU member state supervisory body under the eIDAS Regulation. Only QTSPs can issue qualified electronic signatures (QES), which carry the same legal weight as handwritten signatures throughout the EU. This designation is not self-certified — it requires formal government authorization and ongoing audits.

**What is eHerkenning and who needs it?**
eHerkenning is the Dutch government's business identity scheme, required for companies and organizations to log into Dutch government digital services and portals. Digidentity is one of a small number of authorized eHerkenning providers, making it the go-to choice for organizations needing access to Dutch public sector systems.

**What is NFC passport chip reading and what advantages does it provide?**
NFC chip reading extracts cryptographically signed biographic and biometric data directly from the RFID chip in modern passports and identity cards. Because the chip data cannot be altered or forged (unlike the printed page), this provides higher assurance than optical document scanning alone — relevant for high-value financial transactions or regulated onboarding.

**Is Digidentity's Adobe Approved Trust List (AATL) membership significant?**
AATL membership means Digidentity's digital certificates are trusted by Adobe Acrobat and Reader by default, allowing documents signed with Digidentity-issued certificates to display as verified in Adobe products without additional configuration — practically important for document workflows in legal, financial, and HR contexts.

**How does Digidentity's zero-review status affect procurement decisions?**
With 0 published reviews on any major platform, buyers cannot rely on peer experience for validation. This means procurement must include direct reference checks with named existing customers, a structured POC with defined acceptance criteria, and contractual protections for service levels before go-live.
