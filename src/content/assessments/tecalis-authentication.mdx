## PrimeBiometry Assessment

Tecalis Authentication is a multi-factor authentication product from Tecalis, a Spain-based company with deep roots in European digital trust infrastructure. The product's eIDAS compliance is its primary differentiator in the European market — eIDAS (Electronic Identification, Authentication and Trust Services) is the EU regulatory framework governing electronic signatures and identity services, and compliance with it is a prerequisite for many regulated digital transactions in Europe. Tecalis has operated since 2008, giving it a 16-year track record unusual among biometric authentication vendors. No published reviews exist; buyers should request EU financial services references.

### Best For

European banks, insurance companies, telecom operators, and public sector organizations that need biometric authentication meeting eIDAS requirements for qualified electronic signatures or regulated digital onboarding. Particularly suited to organizations serving customers across multiple EU member states.

### Avoid If

US-headquartered companies with no EU operations, or organizations whose compliance requirements center on SOC 2, HIPAA, or CCPA attestations. Tecalis's compliance framework is built around European regulatory standards, not North American ones.

## FAQ

**What is eIDAS and why does it matter for authentication?**
eIDAS is the EU regulation establishing standards for electronic identification and trust services across member states. Products certified or compliant with eIDAS can be used for legally binding digital transactions, qualified electronic signatures, and regulated customer identification — requirements that standard MFA products do not meet.

**Does Tecalis Authentication support knowledge-based authentication alongside biometrics?**
Yes. The product combines knowledge-based authentication (KBA), facial biometrics, and anti-spoofing controls as a layered MFA approach. This is appropriate for regulated onboarding where multiple authentication factors strengthen non-repudiation.

**What biometric modalities does Tecalis Authentication use?**
The primary biometric modality is facial biometrics with anti-spoofing controls to prevent presentation attacks. Confirm with the vendor whether additional modalities (voice, fingerprint) are available for specific use cases.

**What does "omnichannel integration" mean for Tecalis Authentication?**
Tecalis supports authentication across web, mobile app, and potentially in-branch channels — enabling a consistent identity verification experience regardless of how a customer interacts with the organization.

**Does Tecalis Authentication support KYB (Know Your Business) in addition to KYC?**
KYB compliance is listed as a feature alongside KYC. This is relevant for financial institutions that must verify both individual customers and business entities, though buyers should confirm the depth of business verification capabilities during evaluation.
