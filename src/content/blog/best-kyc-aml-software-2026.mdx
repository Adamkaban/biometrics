## What is KYC/AML Software?

**KYC/AML software** is the technology layer your compliance program runs on. It automates two connected but distinct processes: identity verification (KYC, or Know Your Customer) and money laundering risk screening (AML, or Anti-Money Laundering).

At the core, these platforms do four things: verify that a customer's ID document is genuine, confirm the person presenting it is physically present (liveness detection), check that person against sanctions lists, PEP databases, and adverse media sources, and flag or block high-risk transactions after onboarding.

What KYC/AML software does NOT do is run your compliance program for you. It won't write your policies, train your staff, or make final decisions on complex cases. A platform can surface a sanctions match; your compliance team still has to investigate and escalate. Treat it as a tool, not a substitute for a qualified compliance function.

Typical use cases: fintech customer onboarding (lending, payments, neobanks), bank account opening, crypto exchange verification under VASP regulations, insurance and healthcare identity checks, and marketplace fraud prevention.

Core capabilities covered by these platforms: document capture and verification, biometric liveness detection, AML and sanctions screening, PEP database checks, adverse media monitoring, and, in some cases, ongoing transaction monitoring. Not every platform covers all of these -- that distinction matters when you're evaluating vendors.

## How We Evaluated These Tools

This comparison covers 10 of the most-reviewed KYC and AML platforms in our directory of 60+ vendors. **James Whitfield** evaluated each platform against the following criteria while building PrimeBiometry's vendor database. Ratings combine published G2/Capterra data with technical and pricing analysis.

**1. ID document coverage:** The number of countries and document types supported. Breadth matters if you have international users. Gaps in coverage mean manual review queues.

**2. Liveness detection standard:** iBeta certification level is the benchmark buyers should ask about specifically. iBeta Level 2 is the highest commercially available certification for passive liveness detection. If a vendor can't tell you their iBeta level, that's a red flag.

**3. AML screening:** Which databases are included (OFAC, EU, UN, FATF, local lists), how frequently they update, and whether adverse media screening is bundled or costs extra. Sanctions lists change daily -- update frequency is a real operational risk.

**4. API and SDK quality:** REST API design, mobile SDK availability (iOS, Android, React Native, Flutter), webhook support, and documentation quality. Poor developer experience drives integration delays and ongoing maintenance overhead.

**5. Compliance certifications:** ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, eIDAS, and FedRAMP. These are often hard requirements in enterprise procurement, especially for banking. Ask for the certificates directly -- "working toward SOC 2" means uncertified.

**6. Pricing transparency:** Can you get a realistic price without a sales call? Vendors who publish pricing save you days of procurement overhead. Vendors who hide pricing are often pricing opportunistically.

**7. Support quality:** SLA commitments, documentation depth, availability of dedicated account management. At production scale, slow support is a compliance risk -- verification outages mean business stops.

**8. User reviews:** G2 and Capterra ratings, weighted toward review volume. A 5.0 from 10 reviews is less meaningful than a 4.6 from 112. We note both rating and review count throughout.

## KYC Software Pricing Overview

KYC software pricing works in one of three models: per-verification, subscription, or enterprise contract.

**Per-verification:** Ranges from $0.50 to $2.00 per completed check, depending on what's included. At the low end ($0.05 to $0.50), you're typically getting document scanning only. At the high end ($1.50 to $2.00), you're getting full KYC with biometric liveness plus AML screening in a single call. iDenfy at $1.35 per verified check (with no charge for failures) is at the stronger end of the value range.

**Subscription entry points:** $49 to $209/month for self-serve tiers from Veriff, Ondato, and Entrust IDV. These work for predictable low-to-mid volumes but can get expensive if your monthly verification count is variable.

**Enterprise contracts:** Custom pricing kicks in at roughly 10,000 verifications/month. At that volume, per-verification rates can drop to $0.30 to $0.60 with a committed annual contract. The savings are significant -- but require 12-month commitments and minimum volume guarantees.

For a full cost breakdown including calculation examples, comparison of per-verification vs subscription economics, and volume discount modeling, see the [KYC Pricing Guide 2026](/blog/kyc-pricing-guide-2026).

## FAQ

### What is KYC/AML software?

KYC/AML software automates customer identity verification and money laundering risk screening. It combines document verification, biometric liveness detection, PEP and sanctions screening, and in some platforms, ongoing transaction monitoring. Regulated businesses, including fintechs, banks, and crypto exchanges, use these platforms to meet legal onboarding requirements and reduce fraud at account opening.

One important clarification: KYC/AML software is a technology layer, not a compliance program.
